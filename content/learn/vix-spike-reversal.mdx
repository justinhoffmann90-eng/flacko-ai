## Fear Has a Shelf Life

The VIX — Wall Street's "fear gauge" — measures expected volatility over the next 30 days. When it spikes, the market is pricing in chaos. But here's what the data shows: the chaos doesn't last, and TSLA tends to rip off the bottom.

The VIX Spike Reversal setup fires when the VIX surges **30% or more in a single week**. That's not a normal bad week — that's a liquidation event, a macro shock, or a genuine panic. And historically, those panics mark the point where the selling is nearly done.

---

## The Setup

**Trigger:** VIX weekly change ≥ 30%

**Hold window:** 5 trading days from trigger

**Re-spike reset:** If the VIX spikes another 30%+ while the window is active, the 5-day clock resets. The setup stays live as long as fear keeps escalating.

**Type:** Buy signal

**Framework:** Fixed-horizon (5-day evaluation window)

## Why It Works

When the VIX spikes 30%+ in a week, several things are happening simultaneously:

- **Forced selling** — margin calls, risk-off liquidations, and panic exits flood the market with supply
- **Options repricing** — implied volatility explodes, making puts expensive and calls cheap relative to realized outcomes
- **Mean reversion** — extreme fear readings historically compress back to normal within 2-4 weeks
- **Institutional repositioning** — smart money uses the dislocation to build positions at dislocated prices

TSLA, with its high beta and retail-heavy ownership, tends to overshoot on the downside during these events — and then snap back harder than the broader market.

## What This Is NOT

**This is not a "buy the dip" signal.** Not every red day triggers this. The VIX needs to spike 30%+ in a week — that's a statistically rare event that filters out normal pullbacks and only captures genuine fear events.

**This is not a standalone signal.** The Orb evaluates this alongside 17 other setups. A VIX spike firing during a confirmed downtrend (Dual LL active, EMAs breaking down) carries different weight than one firing during a healthy uptrend pullback.

**The 200 SMA matters.** If TSLA is trading below its 200-day simple moving average when this fires, the statistical edge is weaker. Structural damage can override the mean-reversion tendency.
