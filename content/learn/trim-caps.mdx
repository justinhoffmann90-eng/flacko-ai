## Why Trim Caps Matter

Most traders have buy rules but no sell discipline. Trim caps solve that by defining exactly how much to reduce at each resistance level.

## T-Level Hierarchy (Regime A)

Use this sequence when market is recovering but still below some key EMAs.

| T-Level | Anchor |
|---------|--------|
| T1 | Daily 9 EMA reclaim |
| T2 | Key Gamma Strike reclaim |
| T3 | Daily 21 EMA reclaim |
| T4 | Hedge Wall |
| T5 | Weekly 9 EMA |
| T6 | Weekly 13 EMA |
| T7 | Weekly 21 EMA |
| T8 | Prior swing high |

---

## T-Level Hierarchy (Regime B)

When price is already above all key EMAs (strong uptrend), hierarchy shifts:

| Priority | Anchor |
|----------|--------|
| 1 | Hedge Wall |
| 2 | Most recent swing high |
| 3 | Major round numbers |
| 4 | EMA Extension Zone (% above W9 EMA) |
| 5 | Call Wall |
| 6 | All-time high / 52-week high |

This prevents over-reliance on reclaim logic once trend strength is already established.

---

## EMA Extension Trimming

If price extends far above Weekly 9 EMA, trim discipline becomes more important even in bullish mode.

| Extension Above W9 EMA | Guidance |
|------------------------|----------|
| ~12% | Start adding extra trims |
| ~18% | Increase trim aggressiveness |
| ~25% | Prioritize heavy risk reduction |

(Updated thresholds: **12% / 18% / 25%**)

## Key Takeaway

Trim caps are your anti-emotion sell system:
- Mode determines trim speed
- Regime determines level order
- Extension determines extra urgency
