## Why weekly EMAs matter

Daily charts are noisy. You can find a reason to be bullish or bearish on any given day.

Weekly charts cut through the noise. They show you the **actual trend** ‚Äî the direction that matters over weeks and months, not hours.

## Reading the 9/21 structure

**Strong uptrend:**
- Price above both EMAs
- 9 EMA above 21 EMA
- Pullbacks find support at 9 EMA

**Weakening uptrend:**
- Price drops below 9 EMA
- Still above 21 EMA
- Warning sign, not yet broken

**Trend break:**
- Price below both EMAs
- 9 EMA crosses below 21 EMA
- Trend has changed. Adjust positioning.

## The 21 EMA as the line in the sand

When in doubt, look at the weekly 21 EMA.

**Above it?** The trend is intact. Buy dips. Hold positions. Stay offensive.

**Below it?** The trend is broken or breaking. Reduce size. Get defensive. Wait for reclaim.

This one line answers 80% of "should I be long right now?" questions.

## EMA crosses

When the 9 EMA crosses the 21 EMA, pay attention:

**Bullish cross (9 crosses above 21):**
- Momentum shifting positive
- Often marks the start of a new uptrend
- Confirmation to get more aggressive

**Bearish cross (9 crosses below 21):**
- Momentum shifting negative  
- Often marks the start of a downtrend
- Signal to get defensive

## How we use EMAs in the mode

Weekly EMAs are a key input to the mode calculation:

- **Price above both EMAs** ‚Üí Supports green/yellow modes
- **Price between EMAs** ‚Üí Caution flag, often yellow/orange
- **Price below both EMAs** ‚Üí Strong red mode bias

The EMAs don't override everything, but they anchor the bigger picture.

## Weekly vs daily

Use weekly EMAs for **trend direction** (should I be long at all?).

Use daily levels for **execution** (where do I enter/exit today?).

They work together. Weekly tells you the bias. Daily tells you the levels.
