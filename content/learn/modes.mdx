## What a mode means

A mode is the system's risk dial. It tells you **how aggressive or defensive to be** based on current market conditions. When the mode changes, your **position sizing and expectations** change with it.

## How to use modes in real time

**1. Size first.** Check the mode before planning any trade. Your daily cap is the ceiling.

**2. Quality over quantity.** Lower modes demand higher conviction setups. Don't force trades.

**3. Combine with levels.** Mode tells you *how much*. Levels tell you *where*. Use both.

**4. Re-evaluate after alerts.** When a key level is hit, reassess. Mode + alert = context for action.

## Examples

- **Green mode + bullish alert**: Scale in confidently. Hold through minor noise.
- **Orange mode + bearish alert**: Quick, high-probability moves only. Tight stops.
- **Red mode**: Capital preservation is the priority. Trade small or stand aside.

When you match your risk to the environment, you stop getting chopped up in hostile conditions and maximize gains when conditions favor you.
